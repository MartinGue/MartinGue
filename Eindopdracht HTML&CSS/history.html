Early history

On October 27, 1275, Count Floris V granted toll-free access to the people at the dam in the Amstel. The Toll Privilege from 1275 is the oldest written piece mentioning Amsterdam.

Amsterdam is the capital and largest city of the Netherlands. It has a rich history that spans several centuries. The origins of Amsterdam can be traced back to the 12th century, when a small fishing village called "Aemstelredamme" was established on the banks of the Amstel River.

In the following centuries, Amsterdam grew in size and importance, becoming a major center for trade and commerce. In the 14th and 15th centuries, Amsterdam was a small but prosperous city, known for its brewery, textile, and shipbuilding industries. In the 16th century, Amsterdam experienced a period of rapid growth and expansion, becoming one of the most important cities in Europe. This was a time of great prosperity and cultural flowering, known as the Dutch Golden Age. Amsterdam was a major center of trade and commerce, with a large port and a thriving merchant class. It was also a center of art, science, and culture, with many famous artists, such as Rembrandt and Vermeer, working and living in the city.

17th-18th-19th Century


In the 17th century, Amsterdam continued to flourish as a major center of trade and commerce. The Dutch East India Company, which was based in Amsterdam, became one of the most powerful companies in the world, and Amsterdam was a major center of the global trade in spices, textiles, and other goods.
In the 18th and 19th centuries, Amsterdam's importance as a center of trade and commerce began to decline, but the city continued to grow and evolve. The industrial revolution brought new industries and new forms of transportation to the city, and Amsterdam became a major center of banking, insurance, and other services.

Last Centuries


In the 20th century, Amsterdam faced a number of challenges, including the devastation of World War II and the decline of traditional industries. However, the city has also undergone a process of revitalization and renewal, becoming a major center of tourism, culture, and innovation. Today, Amsterdam is a vibrant, cosmopolitan city that is known for its rich history, culture, and architecture.
