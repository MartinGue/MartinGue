Amsterdam: The Surprising City

Amsterdam, the capital city of the Netherlands, is known for its picturesque canals, historical architecture, and vibrant cultural scene. However, there is much more to this city than meets the eye. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, here are some surprising facts about Amsterdam that are sure to leave you in awe.


Amsterdam is the cycling capital of the world. With over 800,000 bicycles and only 700,000 residents, Amsterdam is the world's leading city for cycling. The city is designed with cyclists in mind, with bike lanes and bike parks at every turn. In fact, cycling is so popular in Amsterdam that it is considered the most bicycle-friendly city in the world.


Amsterdam is home to the world's first stock exchange. Established in 1720, the Amsterdam Stock Exchange was the first in the world and played a significant role in the development of modern finance. Today, the building that once housed the stock exchange serves as the home of the national historical museum, the Amsterdam Museum.


Amsterdam is built on poles. The city of Amsterdam is built on poles, and the ground is below sea level. To protect against floods, the city has an elaborate system of dikes, dams, and canals. In fact, one-third of the city is actually below sea level.


Amsterdam is home to the world's only floating flower market. Located in the heart of Amsterdam, the floating flower market is a unique attraction that attracts millions of visitors every year. The market offers a variety of flowers, plants, and bulbs, and is a must-visit for anyone interested in horticulture or just looking for a unique shopping experience.


Amsterdam has its own floating forest. The NEMO Science Museum in Amsterdam is known for its distinctive green roof, which doubles as a floating forest. The rooftop garden is home to over 2,500 plants and trees, and offers stunning views of the city.


Amsterdam was once the center of the world's diamond trade. For over 400 years, Amsterdam was the center of the world's diamond trade, and it remains an important hub for the industry to this day. The city is home to many of the world's leading diamond dealers, and visitors can tour the Diamond Museum to learn more about the history and science of diamonds.


These are just a few of the many surprising facts about Amsterdam that make it a unique and fascinating city. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or just looking for a new adventure, Amsterdam is sure to captivate and inspire.
